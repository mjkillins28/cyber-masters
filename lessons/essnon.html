When crafting a professional business email, proposal, letter, or any formal written/typed form of communication, it is important for the writer to bring their thoughts across in the most fluid way possible while still making everything understandable. Understanding the difference between essential and non-essential clauses will enable you to communicate in a less confusing way. It is an interesting subject, but it is also, in nature, boring. This video will show you what the difference between the two clauses are and how to use them properly by showing depictions of real-life examples. The narrator will explain what the clauses are, and his colleagues will demonstrate an example of a conversation with an essential clause and without it. The same will be done with the non-essential clause. The narrator will then give an overview of the video and take-away notes.
